# 6-Week Workout Split Plan
**Profile:** Female | 5'2" | Goal: Cut + Body Recomposition (lose fat, build muscle)  
**Schedule:** 5 days/week | 30–45 min strength + 20 min cardio  
**Split:** 2x Legs | 2x Upper Body | 1x Full Body

---

## Weekly Schedule Template

| Day | Focus | Cardio |
|-----|-------|--------|
| Monday | Legs A (Quad-dominant) | 20 min Stairmaster/Elliptical/Bike |
| Tuesday | Upper Body A (Push) | 20 min Stairmaster/Elliptical/Bike |
| Wednesday | REST or light walk | — |
| Thursday | Legs B (Hamstring/Glute-dominant) | 20 min Stairmaster/Elliptical/Bike |
| Friday | Upper Body B (Pull) | 20 min Stairmaster/Elliptical/Bike |
| Saturday | Full Body | 20 min Stairmaster/Elliptical/Bike |
| Sunday | REST | — |

> **Cardio note:** Stairmaster burns the most calories and doubles as glute work. Elliptical is lower impact. Bike is great for active recovery. Mix as desired. Aim for moderate intensity (RPE 6–7 out of 10).

---

## Core Sequence (finish every session — ~8 min)

Perform circuit-style, minimal rest between exercises.

| Exercise | Sets x Reps/Time |
|----------|-----------------|
| Dead Bug | 3 x 10 reps (each side) |
| Hollow Body Hold | 3 x 20–30 sec |
| Bicycle Crunches | 3 x 20 reps |
| Plank (forearm) | 3 x 30–45 sec |
| Side Plank (each side) | 2 x 20–30 sec |

> Progression: Add time/reps by 5 sec or 2 reps every 2 weeks.

---

## Progressive Overload Notes

- **Weeks 1–2:** Learn movements, focus on form. Use lighter weights, higher reps (12–15).
- **Weeks 3–4:** Increase weight slightly, aim for 10–12 reps. Push closer to failure (1–2 reps in reserve).
- **Weeks 5–6:** Heavier loads, 8–10 reps. Aim to beat previous week's weights by at least 2.5 lbs where possible.

## Nutrition Tips for Cut + Muscle Gain (Body Recomposition)

- **Calories:** Aim for a slight deficit (~200–300 cal below maintenance). At 5'2", this is roughly 1,500–1,700 cal/day depending on activity.
- **Protein:** Prioritize 0.8–1g per lb of goal body weight. Protein protects muscle during a cut.
- **Carbs:** Eat most carbs around workouts (pre/post). Oats, rice, fruit, sweet potato.
- **Fats:** Don't cut fat too low — hormones need it. Avocado, nuts, olive oil.
- **Hydration:** Minimum 64–80 oz water/day. More on training days.

## 6-Week Progression Summary

| Week | Intensity | Rep Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | Light–Moderate | 13–15 | Learn movements, build habit |
| 2 | Moderate | 12–13 | Start pushing weight up |
| 3 | Moderate–Heavy | 11–12 | Increase load, reduce rest slightly |
| 4 | Heavy | 10–11 | Focus on progressive overload |
| 5 | Heavy | 9–10 | Compound lifts heavier |
| 6 | Peak week | 8–10 | Max effort — celebrate progress |

---

*Rest days: Walk, stretch, or do light yoga. Sleep 7–9 hours — this is when the muscle actually builds.*
